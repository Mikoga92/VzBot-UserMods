My goal was it to make the least effort and probably cheapest triple z axis mod to the VzBot possible. For this I mostly reused the stock VzBot mount for the motors and linear rods. Only in the back on top I needed to model my own linear rod mounts because otherwise the printhead could crash into the mount.

Things you can reuse:
- 2x 2020 extrusion from the stock X5SA hotbed, just shorten them to be around 330mm
- lead screw bearings from stock X5SA hotbed mount
- 6x linear rod mount from VzBot
- 2x z stepper motor mount, just place them on the front extrusion around 3-5mm from the side extrusion

Things you need:

- ~11x M3 heat inserts with standard Voron specs (M3x5x4)
- ~23x M3 screws with different lengths ranging from 6mm to 20. Not so sure about this because I made some changes to the model after building it.
- ~12x M3 hammer nuts (not sure whether they are called this)
- 1x stepper motor like the other tronxy motors
- 1x stepper motor mount for z axis from VzBot
- 1x T8 lead screw coupler
- 1x T8 lead screw
- 1x T8 flange lead screw
- ~10x M3 nuts, preferably stop nuts or standard nuts and loctite
- 1x angle grinder to shorten the linear rods and the lead screw
- 1x file for finishing the cut on the linear rods and lead screw

That should be almost everything, probably I forgot something...

Files to print:

- 2x 'Flexure + Right Mount'. You need 2 of them. 1 normal and 1 mirrored. Just mirror it in slicer and print.
- 2x Bearing Mount. Also the 2nd needs to be mirrored.
- 1x Flexure + Back mount
- 1x Linear Rod Mount Back

Print everything with at least 4 walls.

Preferred material is ABS or ASA. PETG should work too I think.

Infill doesn't come much into play I think. I printed it with 10% Gyroid.

In slicer just import the files and split them to parts or objects so you can orient them automatically. Everything is printable without supports.
